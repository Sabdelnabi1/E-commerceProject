What makes Bali so special?

Beyond stunning beaches and magical temples, Bali has virtually every kind of natural beauty. Glorious mountainous areas with lush greenery, scenic lakes, gorgeous waterfalls, iconic rice fields, flower gardens, gushing sacred rivers and secret canyons all make up the island's landscape.



Fun Facts About Bali


Kopi Luwak

Bali of the few places around the world where you can buy authentic Kopi Luwak – one of the most expensive coffees in the world. This $50 cup of coffee is made from the droppings of the Luwak, a shy forest animal as its body can not digest coffee.

Black Sand Beach

Bali is also home to incredibly stunning black volcanic sand beaches. Courtesy of the active volcanoes that dot the island and the Indonesian Peninsula. The black sand is believed to be sacred; a gift from the gods infused with the fiery power of volcanic lava.

Temples

Known as the Island of a Thousand Temples, Bali houses over 20,000 temples. Every village has at least three principal temples, called Pura Puseh, Pura Desa and Pura Dalem. All temples are positioned in the direction of Mount Agung so they can pray to the gods.
